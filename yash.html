Sir C.V. Raman

The man who discovered ground-breaking light-scattering effect,Raman effect.


Sir C.V. Raman a pioneering indian scientist.

Here's a time line of his life.

  • 1888 - Born in Madaras Precedency,India.
  • 1907 - Married to Lokasundari Ammal
  • 1916 - Professor at B.H.U.
  • 1917 -Resigned from government service and joined Indian Association for the Cultivation of Science. He described this as his golden era.
  • 1928 - Conducted experiments at IACS along with his fellow K.S. Krishnan, on the scattering of light, which later came to be known as Raman Effect.
  • 1929 - He was the pesident of Indian Science Congress. He was offered knoghthood ad honary doctrates from various universities.
  • 1930 - He was awarded the Nobel prize for his work on scattering of light, known as Raman Effect. He was so confident of winning the Nobel Prize that he booked the tockets in july , even when the results were to be announced in november. He used to look in newspapers for any announcement.
  • 1932 - He was succeded by Debendra Mohan Bose as Palit professor.
  • 1933 - He left IACS and joined as IISc, Bangalore as first indian director.
  • 1941 - Awarded Franklin Medal.
  • 1954 - Awarded Bharat Ratna.
  • 1970 - He died peacefully.
